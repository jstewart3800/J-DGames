Number 1 Pick

Mortal Kombat is my favorite franchise. However, if I have to choose one, I would have to go with the tenth installment of the series. Mortal Kombat 9 was NRS's (Nether Realm Studio) reboot of the series. Mortal Kombat X took all that was good from Mortal Kombat 9 and made it bigger, darker, faster, and just a whole lot of fun. The game delivered a stellar story mode, this time introducing a new generation of heroes including Cassie Cage (daughter of Johnny and Sonya), Jacqui Briggs (daughter of Jax) and Takeda (son of Kenshi, student of Scorpion). The new variation system provided three unique play styles per character, while the return of the Run button and interactive environments made fights more dynamic than ever.

Number 2 Pick

Overwatch is a team-based strategy first person shooter game. Wow that is a lot of genres mashed into one game. This game took a page from Team Fortress 2's book. I've got a lot of love for this game and I also have a lot of hate for this game. You are in a team of six heros where you can be either a tank, a healer, or a damage dealer. There are several types of objectives, king of the hill, escort the payload, a combination of the two, or capture the point. Either way you have it you trying to kill the enemy team. My favorite hero is Ana, she's a tiny fierce old lady that shoots her own teammates to heal them (uh what? like don't shoot your darts at me lady while we are waging war over here). Overwatch is right behind League of Legends in the Esports world. It is a great game, a fantastic shooter, and cleverly designed.

Number 3 Pick

The Zelda franchise is one of Nintendo's biggest success. In this instance of the series Link wakes up after being asleep for 100 years, because of course Gannon is back at it again. When you wake up you can't remember anything. So you pretty much just go out in the world and figure everything out on your own. There are no tutorials, there's no "Navi" telling me to listen up (I can hear her now, "Hey Listen") to tell me where to go. The gameplay is amazing, if you play for 100 hours you'll still probably find something new that you haven't descovered yet. The puzzles are classic. The graphics are phenominal. Breath of the Wild can be played over and over again. And the fights...the fighting system is where this game really shines. You can fight on horseback. You can freeze objects in time, hit them, and make it fly into the enemy, you can do Metal Gear Solid style and sneak around to kill. The list goes on and on.

Number 4 Pick

World of Warcraft is what's known as an mmorpg. It was the first mmo I had ever played. World of Warcraft ate up a lot of my time. It was a big reason I didn't make it out of college. The game was so much fun and so addicting I didn't care about studying or grades. The grind, it was all about the grind. Getting that oh so satisfying "Ding"! Earning bigger better gear, making your hero the most badass dude in Azeroth. I had never expericenced anything like that before. Part of the game was the commutity. You will be in a certain area and see someone doing the same quest as you and you would just team up. Next thing you know two hours have past hanging out with someone you've never talked to before. Although I put the game down I had to include it on my top list as respect for what a great game Blizzard put out.

Number 5 Pick

Contra is a single or co-op player side scroller. Contra is more of an all-time nostalgic pick for me. My dad and I still pick up the game every once in a while to run through it and get some good laughs. I've always thought of it as a perfect game to learn how to play games. Aim, dodge, run, jump. The program never throws anything new at you, and the levels generate the same enemies everytime. That being said there still is a lot of difficulty to the game. There are a lot of cool upgrades to your weapon. Contra also has the most famous cheat code of all time. Up, up, down, down, left, right, left, right, B, A, Select, Start. The code gives you 30 lives to get through six levels to take down the Red Falcon, which has something to do with aliens. The story never was the greatest, but still a great game in general.
