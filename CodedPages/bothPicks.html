Number 1 Pick

Link to the Past: There are few series that have stood the test of time like the Legend of Zelda, and nearly all are shining examples of the franchise, but none are so deserving of the top spot on this list than A Link to the Past. Where the original Legend of Zelda made history with its open world and spirit of adventure, A Link to the Past builds off of that formula and establishes staples of the series that are still around today, and for good reason. A Link to the Past gave us our first taste of what a fully realized Hyrule could feel like, with an expansive world to explore and a good sense of direction for when it's time to get to business, one of the most responsive and satisfying combat systems in gaming, and dozens of fascinating gadgets and tools that make puzzles and exploration a joy to complete. It's no wonder that A Link to the Past is still highly regarded as one of the best games of all time.

Number 2 Pick

The original Castlevania is an experience every gamer should have. Castlevania throws the player into a dark and unforgiving world of ghoulish horrors placed expertly in the player's way to make the player rethink their approach to each challenge. This combines seamlessly with the weapons and tools at the player's disposal to create an ever-present sense of tension and urgency that makes the player feel like they're really in the castle, surrounded by danger. With iconic music and beautiful level design, everything about this progenitor to one of gaming's most beloved series gives the player an immense and palpable sense of fear and determination. Few other games accomplish so tangible an experience to this day, especially considering it came out more than 30 years ago.

Number 3 Pick

Final Fantasy is a series that's known for its unrivaled world-building and sense of adventure, and Final Fantasy 7 is one of its most shining examples of this. With its complex turn-based combat system, players are given a taste of what it's like to really have to decide what to do in a fight, since your turn was literally on a timer. With every place you visit and every character you meet, the player is faced with the grand scope of just how important their quest really is. Final Fantasy 7 introduces a depth of story and emotion that was unparalleled at the time, and even now few games can boast the same kind of development in their characters and stories. There are good reasons why everyone wants this game to be remade so badly.

Number 4 Pick

Yall remember Tricky, right? The physics-defying super moves ought to be enough to jog the memory, with characters spinning their boards in front of them like a propeller, or doing the worm in midair, but where Tricky really shines is in its sound design. From that smooth announcer voice telling you just how much you rule/suck, to that beautiful “Trickaaay!” when you fill your uber meter, and who could forget the dulcet tones of Run D.M.C! Tricky's level designs featured a vibrant pallete and futuristic theme with interesting objects and collectables that fly past at top speed while you're roaring down the mountain. From top to bottom, SSX Tricky is non-stop fun steeped in skill and an emphasis on styling on everyone around you.

Number 5 Pick

Since the dawn of time, humanity has always strived to answer life's most vexing questions, but only one has weathered the storms of time to continue to vex us to this day; who would win in a fight between Nintendo's coolest characters? From those of us who started way back on the N64 to the newcomers on the switch, Super Smash Brothers has been the quintessential party and tournament game for decades, and it all started with the low-poly classic of 1999. Super Smash Brothers is the paradigm in cartoon violence, with an emphasis on tight controls, interesting stages and items, and a determination to master the art of beating your friends into the dirt with some of Nintendo's most family-friendly characters turned into blood-thirsty murderers, all in the name of fun!
